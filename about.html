Research Philosophy

My research addresses a fundamental challenge in AI: How can we make advanced capabilities accessible without massive computational resources? While large models demonstrate remarkable performance, their deployment costs create barriers to innovation and practical adoption.

I develop methods that enable smaller, efficient models to learn from larger ones without expensive annotations—making state-of-the-art AI accessible to researchers and practitioners with limited resources. Equally important is ensuring these systems are fair and robust, which drives my work on debiasing in limited data settings.

Current Focus

At Imperial College London, I'm exploring:

  • Label-free model alignment: Enabling small VLMs to learn from large models through reasoning-based distillation
  • Efficient multimodal systems: Building unified frameworks that work across vision, language, and other modalities
  • Practical bias mitigation: Developing debiasing methods that work without explicit annotations or large datasets
